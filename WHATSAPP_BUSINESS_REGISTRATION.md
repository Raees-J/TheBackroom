## Option 1: Register as a Sole Proprietor (Easiest for South Africa)

### What is a Sole Proprietor?
A sole proprietorship is the simplest business structure where you operate as an individual. In South Africa, you don't need to register with CIPC if your annual turnover is below R1 million.

### Steps to Register:

#### 1. Register with SARS (South African Revenue Service)
**Required Documents:**
- Your ID document (certified copy)
- Proof of residential address (utility bill, bank statement - not older than 3 months)
- Bank account details

**Process:**
1. Visit your nearest SARS branch or go online: https://www.sarsefiling.co.za
2. Register as an individual taxpayer (if not already registered)
3. Apply for an Income Tax Number
4. Request a Tax Clearance Certificate (optional but helpful)

**Cost:** FREE
**Time:** 1-2 weeks

#### 2. Open a Business Bank Account
**Required Documents:**
- Your ID document
- Proof of residence
- SARS Income Tax Number
- Business description/plan (simple document explaining The Backroom)

**Recommended Banks:**
- Capitec Business Account (Low fees, easy setup)
- FNB Business Account
- Standard Bank Business Account

**Cost:** R0-R100/month depending on bank
**Time:** 1-2 days

#### 3. Get a Business Letter/Proof
Create a simple business document with:
- Your business name: "The Backroom"
- Your name as owner
- Business address
- Contact details (phone, email)
- Business description
- Date started

**Template provided below**

## Option 2: Register a Company with CIPC (More Formal)

### What is CIPC?
Companies and Intellectual Property Commission - South Africa's official business registration body.

### Types of Registration:

#### A. Private Company (Pty Ltd)
**Best for:** Growing businesses, multiple owners, liability protection

**Required Documents:**
1. Certified copy of ID for all directors
2. Proof of residential address for all directors
3. Company name reservation (CK1 form)
4. Memorandum of Incorporation (MOI)
5. Notice of Incorporation (CoR 14.1)

**Process:**
1. Reserve company name online: https://eservices.cipc.co.za
2. Complete incorporation documents
3. Submit to CIPC
4. Receive registration certificate

**Cost:** R175 (name reservation) + R125 (registration) = R300
**Time:** 5-10 business days

#### B. Close Corporation (CC)
**Note:** No longer available for new registrations (phased out in 2011)

## Common Questions

### Q: Can I use my personal ID for business verification?
**A:** Yes, as a sole proprietor, your personal ID is your business ID.

### Q: Do I need a physical office?
**A:** No, you can use your home address as your business address.

### Q: What if Meta rejects my verification?
**A:** You can resubmit with additional documents or appeal the decision.

### Q: How long does verification take?
**A:** Usually 2-5 business days, but can take up to 2 weeks.

### Q: Can I start using WhatsApp Business API before verification?
**A:** You can use test numbers, but need verification for production use.
